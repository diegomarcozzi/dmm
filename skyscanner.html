Skyscanner

Overview

I received an invitation from Skyscanner (EMEA) to design a responsive ad campaign to be displayed through the Google Display Network. This work was entirely done through Upwork (contract #21632167, Feb 2019).

My Role

Lead Product Designer

Hats worn

Team Leader Product Design Interaction Design Design Thinking Wireframing Prototyping UI/UX Complex Problem Solving Mentoring Conceptual Thinking Art Direction

The Challenge

As the lead of a team of 2 designers, our main task was to create a responsive template that could be effectively applied to the over 20 different banner types available on the Google Display Network. Despite its apparent simplicity, this project presented several challenges, particularly in regards to smaller banner sizes and ensuring readability. Additionally, we had to consider the brand guidelines, including the logo placement and background, and accommodate the client's request for the ability to edit prices. It's worth noting that each destination presented unique design challenges, so our designs needed to be flexible and adaptable to various contexts. To complete this project, our team utilized Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop, and Google Web Designer.

Sometimes you have to work a lot to achieve a concept that convinces everyone. In this case we were very lucky, the marketing team approved one of our first designs, which after some minor modifications was the one chosen for the Skyscanner Summer 2019 Campaign.

I assembled the delivered files in such a way that the price could be easily edited from a single line of html. I could have delivered it as it was, however I went a step further by documenting and adding notes to each file and code segment as the idea was to make it so simple that even a person with no coding experience in their marketing team could be able to edit it in the future.
